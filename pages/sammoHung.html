Sammo Hung





Samuel "Sammo" Hung Kam Bo, born 7 January 1952, is a Hong Kong actor, martial artist, film producer, and film director, known for his work in martial arts films, Hong Kong action cinema, and as a fight choreographer for actors such as Kim Tai Chung, Jackie Chan, Yuen Biao, and Yuen Wah. Throughout the 1980s, he was part of the "Three Dragons" along with Chan and Biao, with the three starring in six Hong Kong films together. Hung is a pivotal figure who spearheaded the Hong Kong New Wave movement of the 1980s, helped reinvent the martial arts genre, and popularized the zombie-like jiangshi genre. He is widely credited with assisting many of his compatriots by giving them their starts in the Hong Kong film industry, either by casting them in the films he produced or by giving them roles in the production crew.
