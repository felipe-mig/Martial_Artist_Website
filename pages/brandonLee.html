Brandon Lee (1965-1993)





Brandon Bruce Lee, born February 1, 1965, and passed away on March 31, 1993, was an American actor. Establishing himself as a rising action star in the early 1990s, he landed his breakthrough role as Eric Draven in the supernatural superhero film The Crow (1994). Tragically, his career and life were cut short by an accidental death during the film's production. The son of martial artist and film star Bruce Lee, who passed away when Brandon was eight years old, Lee followed in his father's footsteps. He trained in martial arts, including Jeet Kune Do, Wing Chun, Eskrima, Silat, and Muay Thai, and studied acting at Emerson College and the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute. Lee's career began with leading roles in the Hong Kong action film Legacy of Rage (1986) and the straight-to-video hit Laser Mission (1989), which found financial success on home video. He also appeared in two spin-offs of the 1970s series Kung Fu: the television film Kung Fu: The Movie (1986) and the pilot Kung Fu: The Next Generation (1987).
